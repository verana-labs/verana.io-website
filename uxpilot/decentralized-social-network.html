The Problem with Today's Social Networks

Traditional social networks are centralized platforms that dictate who can publish, how content is moderated, and how user data is monetized. They come with serious drawbacks:

Data Exploitation

Platforms harvest and sell user data without transparent consent or fair compensation.

Censorship & Bias

Opaque moderation rules are uneven, biased, and controlled by a central authority.

Vendor Lock-in

Creators cannot easily move their audience from one network to another.

Fake Accounts & Bots

Lack of verifiable identity creates fraud and misinformation.

The Result?

A system where users are products, not participants.

Introducing the Verifiable Trust Social Network

Imagine This

A social network where creators and communities own their channels, manage their own moderation, and connect in a network secured by verifiable trust.

Architecture Components

1. Social Channel (Verifiable Service)

A Verifiable Trust Social Network provides an open source Social Channel Verifiable Service (a container). Any influencer, creator, or community can deploy their own channel in the datacenter of their choice.

100% Ownership

Complete ownership of data and followers

Self-Hosted

Channels are not platform-dependent

Signed Content

Cryptographically signed by channel's DID

2. Social Browser (Verifiable User Agent)

A browser and/or mobile app that lets users explore decentralized social channels. Think of it as a social browser: instead of visiting centralized feeds, users navigate across independently hosted channels.

Searchable

Through Verana's DID Directory

Verifiable

Channels present Verifiable Credentials

Portable

No single platform controls visibility

Identity and Trust

To appear in the Social Channel User Agent, channel owners must:

1

Attach Person Credential

Attach a Person Credential (ECS) to their channel with an avatar or identity proof.

2

Obtain Social Channel Credential

Obtain a Social Channel Credential (free or purchased, depends on Social Channel Verifiable User Agent owner) that marks the channel as usable.

3

Register DID

Register their DID in the Verana DID Directory, making the channel indexed and searchable.

Trust Guarantee

This ensures followers know exactly who owns the channel.

Moderation

Channel-Level Responsibility

Each creator moderates their own channel, maintaining full control over their community standards.

Credential-Based Access

Followers may need to present credentials (age proof, membership, verified identity) for deeper engagement.

Credential Revocation

If harmful content is posted by channel owner, or channel not properly moderated by its owner, the ecosystem can revoke the Social Channel Credential, or even the Person Credential.

Bottom-Up Moderation

This creates a bottom-up, verifiable moderation system instead of opaque centralized censorship.
