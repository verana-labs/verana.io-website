The Trust Problem

Today, the internet still lacks a native verifiable trust layer. Content, services, and users interact without cryptographic guarantees of who they are or what they represent. This fuels misinformation, fraud, opaque intermediaries, and a general erosion of trust online.

No Identity Verification

No cryptographic proof of who runs a service, owns content, or controls an account

Fake Identities & Fraud

Brand impersonation, credential stuffing, phishing, and misinformation spread unchecked

Opaque Intermediaries

Users cannot reliably verify identity or authenticity of what they see and who they engage with

How Verana Solves This

Verana introduces a public, permissionless trust infrastructure that generalizes the use of verifiable credentials. Any ecosystem - governments, industries, communities... - can use Verana to build sovereign trust networks.

Within these networks, participants can issue, verify, and obtain credentials that certify claims of identity, authenticity, or authorization. Credential holders can attach these proofs to their digital assets, services, and channels, demonstrating ownership, provenance, or qualifications.

The Result

This creates a Verifiable Identity Layer for the Internet, turning the web into an environment where trust is transparent, interoperable, and verifiable.

Examples of What Becomes Verifiable

With Verana's trust infrastructure, virtually any digital claim can be made verifiable:

Digital Services

A service can prove who is its owner through cryptographically verifiable credentials.

Individual Claims

As an individual, I can prove anything online (identity, membership, diploma, qualifications).

User Agents

Services can verify what software users are connecting with and its authenticity.

Content Provenance

Anyone can verify signed content and gather who is its authentic publisher.

Smart Contracts

Organizations can prove they control specific smart contracts and their operations.

RWA Token Ownership

Individuals can prove ownership of Real World Asset (RWA) tokens with verification.

How Trust Flows in Verana

1. Credential Issuance

Owner requests verification from a trusted issuer and receives a Verifiable Credential (VC)

2. Credential Attachment

Owner attaches the VC to their service, proving ownership and authenticity

3. Local Verification

Users verify the VC locally without contacting the issuer - trust is established

Key Benefits of Local Verification

Check issuer signature cryptographically
Validate VC integrity and expiry status
Use cached keys and trust registries
No contact with the issuer required
