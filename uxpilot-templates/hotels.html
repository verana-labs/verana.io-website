The Problem: Hotel Discovery & Broker Domination

The hotel industry is a striking example of how centralization locks value. Today, nearly all hotel reservations happen through brokering platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda, or Airbnb. These intermediaries have achieved near-monopoly power, capturing a huge share of hotel revenues and indirectly raising room costs for users.

The Paradox

While users believe they're saving money, they actually pay more, since hotels must increase their prices to cover broker fees.

Market Reality

If a hotel isn't listed on broker platforms, it effectively doesn't exist in the market.

Current Centralized Model

Travelers

Search and book through centralized platforms

Brokers

Control visibility and extract high commissions

Hotels

Pay high commissions for visibility

Hotel Management Systems Today

To manage their properties, hotels often rely on Hotel Management Systems (HMS), which may be proprietary (SaaS) or open source (self-hosted/cloud-hosted).

Why Open Source HMS Appeals to Hotels

No Vendor Lock-in

Host software anywhere, maintain full control

Data Ownership

Hotels retain complete ownership of their data

Key Open Source Modules

  • Property Management System (PMS)
  • Booking Engine
  • Hotel Website Builder
  • Hotel Mobile App Skeleton

Current Business Models of Open Source HMS

Software Add-ons

Channel managers to sync with booking platforms

Custom Development

Tailored development services for specific needs

Hosting Solutions

Managed hosting and infrastructure services

The Visibility Problem Remains

These systems work well for hotel management, but visibility is still dictated by brokers. Even when hotels run their own websites/apps, most new customers still arrive via brokers.
