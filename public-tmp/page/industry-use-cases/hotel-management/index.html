The Problem: Hotel Discovery & Broker Domination

The hotel industry is a striking example of how centralization locks value. Nearly every reservation flows through a broker such as Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda, or Airbnb—platforms that capture a huge share of hotel revenues and quietly raise room costs for travellers.

The Paradox

Travellers think they’re saving money, yet hotels must raise prices to cover broker fees.

Market Reality

Brokers mediate discovery—if you’re absent from their listings, you effectively don’t exist.
Hotels surrender pricing power and constant access to guest data.
Commission rates erode margins and fund ever-stronger monopolies.
Guests pay more, yet still rely on opaque reviews and unverifiable claims.

What this creates

  • Hotels pay to be visible while losing control of bookings, data, and customer relationships.
  • Travellers fund the monopoly but receive little verifiable assurance about what they book.

Hotel Management Systems Today

Open-source Hotel Management Systems (HMS) let properties run their own stack—no vendor lock-in, clean data ownership, and modular tooling covering PMS, booking engines, websites, and apps.

How Open HMS Providers Can Use Verana

1. Understand Today’s Landscape

Open-source HMS platforms already offer the modules hotels need—but visibility still depends on centralized brokers.

No Vendor Lock-in

Direct Data Access

Key Modules

PMS, booking engine, website builder, and mobile app skeletons ready to extend.

2. Current Business Models

Open-source vendors monetise today via add-ons, custom development, and hosting—but these do not solve the discovery lock-in.

Software Add-ons

Channel managers that sync inventory back into broker platforms.

Custom Development

Tailored features for hotel groups or specialist operators.

Managed Hosting

Running the HMS stack for properties that don’t want to self-host.

3. Add Verifiable Trust

Blend the HMS stack with Verana so hotels become verifiable services discoverable without brokers.

Create a Hotel Ecosystem with an Ecosystem Governance Framework (EGF) and hotel credential schema.

Integrate Verana’s trust stack into booking engines, PMS, and mobile apps.

Launch a Verifiable User Agent—an HMS Provider app that acts like a broker, minus the rent extraction.
